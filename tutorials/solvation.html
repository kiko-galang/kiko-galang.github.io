Ion Solvation in Concentrated Electrolytes

An interactive tutorial on solvation thermodynamics and activity coefficient corrections

Theory

In concentrated solutions, accounting for solvation is necessary. Solvation adjusts the entropy of mixing because a non-trivial fraction of solvent molecules is removed from the free bulk pool when they form ion hydration shells. Ideal mixing assumes all solvent molecules contribute to the mixing entropy; solvation relaxes this assumption by distinguishing bound vs. unbound solvent and solvated vs. unsolvated ions.

This process is modeled as a series of binding equilibria governed by a microscopic solvent–ion binding constant \( k_i \) and a maximum number of available sites \( \mathcal{N}_i \) on the ion. The contribution of solvation to non-ideality is expressed through the excess Gibbs free energy:

$$ \frac{\Delta G^{\mathrm{slv}}}{RT} = n_0 \ln \alpha_0 + \sum_{i \neq 0} n_i \ln\!\left(\frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_i^{\infty}}\right) $$

Here \( x_0 \) and \( x_i \) are the overall mole fractions of solvent and ion \( i \), while \( x_0^{\mathrm{slv}} \) and \( x_i^{\mathrm{slv}} \) are the mole fractions of the free (unbound) solvent and unsolvated ions.

Average Solvation Number

The average number of solvent molecules bound to ion \( i \) follows a Langmuir-type saturation expression:

$$ \bar{h}_i = \frac{N_i\, k_i\, x_0^{\mathrm{slv}}}{1 + k_i\, x_0^{\mathrm{slv}}} $$

where \( N_i \) is the total number of independent solvation sites and \( k_i \) is the microscopic binding constant at a single site. The animation above shows this saturation in real time — bound waters (glowing) vs. free waters (dim) as you vary \( k \), \( N \), and concentration \( c \).

Correction Factors

The model distinguishes apparent mole fractions (total species, \( x_k \)) from true mole fractions of free species (\( x_k^{\mathrm{slv}} \)). The solvent correction \( \alpha_0 \) accounts for depletion of free solvent into hydration shells:

$$ \alpha_0 = \gamma_0^{\mathrm{slv}} = \frac{x_0^{\mathrm{slv}}}{x_0} = \frac{1 - \displaystyle\sum_i \bar{h}_i \frac{x_i}{x_0}} {x_0 + \displaystyle\sum_i x_i\,(1 - \bar{h}_i)} $$

The solute correction \( \alpha_i \), normalized to its infinite-dilution value \( \alpha_i^\infty \), gives the solvation activity coefficient \( \gamma_i^{\mathrm{slv}} \):

$$ \gamma_i^{\mathrm{slv}} = \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_i^{\infty}} = \frac{(1 + k_i)^{N_i}} {x_0\,(1 + k_i x_0)^{N_i} \left(1 + \displaystyle\sum_{j \neq 0} \frac{x_j}{x_0}(1 - \bar{h}_j)\right)} $$

The system is implicit: \( \bar{h}_i \) depends on \( x_0^{\mathrm{slv}} \), which itself depends on all \( \bar{h}_i \). The animation solves this iteratively (toggle Self-consistent on/off to see the difference from the approximate solution).
