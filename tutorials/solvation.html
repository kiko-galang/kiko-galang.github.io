Theory

In concentrated solutions, accounting for solvation is necessary. Solvation adjusts the entropy of mixing because a non-trivial fraction of solvent molecules is removed from the free bulk pool when they form ion hydration shells. Ideal mixing assumes all solvent molecules contribute to the mixing entropy; solvation relaxes this assumption by distinguishing bound vs. unbound solvent and solvated vs. unsolvated ions.

This process is modeled as a series of binding equilibria governed by a microscopic solvent–ion binding constant \( k_i \) and a maximum number of available sites \( \mathcal{N}_i \) on the ion. The contribution of solvation to non-ideality is expressed through the excess Gibbs free energy:

$$ \frac{\Delta G^{\mathrm{slv}}}{RT} = n_0 \ln \alpha_0 + \sum_{i \neq 0} n_i \ln\!\left(\frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_i^{\infty}}\right) $$

Here \( x_0 \) and \( x_i \) are the overall mole fractions of solvent and ion \( i \), while \( x_0^{\mathrm{slv}} \) and \( x_i^{\mathrm{slv}} \) are the mole fractions of the free (unbound) solvent and unsolvated ions.

Correction Factors

The solvent correction \( \alpha_0 = x_0^{\mathrm{slv}} / x_0 \) accounts for depletion of free solvent into hydration shells:

$$ \alpha_0 = \gamma_0^{\mathrm{slv}} = \frac{x_0^{\mathrm{slv}}}{x_0} = \frac{1 - \displaystyle\sum_i \bar{h}_i \frac{x_i}{x_0}} {x_0 + \displaystyle\sum_i x_i (1 - \bar{h}_i)} $$

The solute correction \( \alpha_i = x_i^{\mathrm{slv}} / x_i \) accounts for the fraction of ions remaining unbound at equilibrium. Normalized to its infinite-dilution value \( \alpha_i^\infty \), it gives the solvation activity coefficient:

$$ \gamma_i^{\mathrm{slv}} = \frac{\alpha_i}{\alpha_i^{\infty}} = \frac{(1 + k_i)^{N_i}} {x_0 \bigl(1 + k_i x_0\bigr)^{N_i} \left(1 + \displaystyle\sum_{j \neq 0} \frac{x_j}{x_0}(1 - \bar{h}_j)\right)} $$

The average number of solvent molecules bound to ion \( i \) is given by the Langmuir-type expression:

$$ \bar{h}_i = \frac{N_i k_i x_0^{\mathrm{slv}}}{1 + k_i x_0^{\mathrm{slv}}} $$

The system is implicit: \( \bar{h}_i \) depends on the free solvent fraction \( x_0^{\mathrm{slv}} \), which itself depends on all \( \bar{h}_i \). This requires iterative solution.
